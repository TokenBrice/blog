The CRV Wars series explains how liquidity incentives became a governance market. It starts with Curve's vote-escrow model, follows the Convex layer, then generalizes the pattern to Balancer and Velodrome-style DEXes.

## Reading Path

1. [CRV wars](/crv-wars/) covers veCRV, Convex, vote buying, bribes, and the race to direct CRV emissions.
2. [Advanced CRV warfare](/crv-wars-l2/) maps the second layer of protocols built around Curve and Convex.
3. [Curve vs Velodrome](/crv-vs-velo/) compares veCRV and veVELO incentives, user experience, and liquidity alignment.
4. [Balancer Wars](/balancer-wars/) extends the liquidity-driver framework beyond Curve.
